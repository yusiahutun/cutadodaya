Ud Las Palmas was founded in 1949.

Ud Las Palmas, one of the most iconic football clubs in Spain, was established in 1949 in the city of Las Palmas de Gran Canaria. Since then, it has become a symbol of pride for the people of Gran Canaria.

The club’s nickname is “Los Amarillos.”

Ud Las Palmas is affectionately known as “Los Amarillos,” which translates to “The Yellows” in English. The nickname originated from the team’s traditional yellow home jersey.

The team plays its home matches at the Estadio Gran Canaria.

Ud Las Palmas calls the Estadio Gran Canaria its home ground. This impressive stadium has a seating capacity of over 30,000 and provides an electric atmosphere for the team’s home matches.

Ud Las Palmas has had many successful seasons in La Liga.

Ud Las Palmas has experienced several successful seasons in La Liga, Spain’s top football division. The club’s best finish came in the 1968-1969 season when they achieved a remarkable third-place finish.

One of the most heated rivalries in Spanish football exists between Ud Las Palmas and CD Tenerife, another team from the Canary Islands. Matches between these two teams, known as the Canary Islands Derby, are always intense and passionate.
