F1 grid walk star Martin Brundle's incredible net worth and how he accumulated it

Sky Sports F1 star Martin Brundle has racked up a considerable net worth from his exploits on and off the track.

Brundle, the fan-favourite commentator, enjoys a lavish life in a village near King's Lynn, Norfolk. According to Celebrity Net Worth, the 63-year-old is valued at a staggering £83.6million ($100m).

He's built up his wealth from his racing career and now his broadcasting ventures. With F1 back for the 2024 season, Daily Star Sport is taking a look at Brundle's life away from the limelight.

House

Martin's home is in Gayton, a cosy village in Norfolk not far from his King's Lynn roots. He's previously described himself as a proud "west Norfolk boy" through and through.

When everyone was stuck at home because of the pandemic, fans saw inside Brundle's study when he popped up on telly and online. He's got a painting on his wall of him racing at the 1992 British Grand Prix, reports The Express.

Cars

Martin chatted to Evo Magazine in 2021 and shared about his amazing car collection. He's got some really cool motors, like his Aston Martin DBS that he totally loves.

But his top pick is his Porsche Cayenne 4. 2 V8 diesel perfect for road trips. Brundle's also had some fancy Ferraris, like the 328 GTS, 355 GTB, and a 550 Maranello.

His very first ride was a Toyota Corolla Coupe SR that he jazzed up with "Wolfrace wheels" when he was just starting out.

Family and friends

Brundle met his wife Liz while they were both students in King's Lynn, and the couple now have two children. Like his dad, their son Alex also made a name for himself in sports car racing and even took part in the famous 24 Hours of Le Mans race.

He also commentates for Sky Sports F1's coverage of F2 and F3 races. Brundle is mates with Channel 4 commentator David Coulthard, and the pair celebrated Martin's 50th birthday last year with a meal out.

Brundle is also part of a group of British drivers called the 'Rat Pack', which includes Damon Hill and the former Top Gear Stig, Perry McCarthy.

Hobbies

Did you know that ex-F1 star Martin is a big music fan? Last summer he posted a clip of him at a Rolling Stones gig in London's Hyde Park.

He thought it was awesome, saying: "The Stones were pure class in Hyde Park. Forget the octogenarian backstory, they are great and energetic musicians and entertainers. Wonderful."

Like most racing drivers, Martin loves anything on wheels and has a soft spot for motorcycles. He told Evo magazine: "I'm also into bikes. I've got a BMW K1600 GT, straight-six across the frame. I ride to a handful of European F1 races each season."

He often promotes motorcycling and talks about why it makes him tick during TV and radio interviews.

Racing

Martin Brundle had an epic fight with Ayrton Senna back in 1983 for the Formula Three title. Senna won, but he still said something mega nice about Martin.

On Instagram, Martin shared: "Ayrton told me at this moment, happy in F3 championship victory in the final few laps, that I was the best British driver since Jim Clark."

Martin then raced in F1 from 1984 and later took a break to race other cool cars. He won the big Le Mans race in 1990 and came back to F1, but never got to win a Grand Prix.

Now, Martin is famous for talking about F1 on telly and sometimes races with his son Alex in old fast cars like the Jaguar E-Type at Silverstone Classic.
