Lisa Hochstein Divorce Settlement From Lenny: Net Worth, RHOM Salary

It’s somewhere up in the air. Lisa Hochstein’s divorce settlement is expanding by the day and onscreen. Lisa joined The Real Housewives of Miami—Bravo’s reality TV series following the personal and professional lives of wealthy women living in Miami, Florida—in Season 2 in 2012.

The series—which is a part of Bravo’s Real Housewives franchise featuring 11 cities across the world—premiered in 2011 and was canceled in 2013. It was rebooted by Peacock, NBC Universal’s streaming service, in 2021 after almost a decade off the air. The Real Housewives of Miami moved from Peacock to Bravo in 2023 in Season 6, which premiered in November 2023.

Related Stories

Lisa and Lenny were a main storyline in The Real Housewives of Miami Season 5 after Lenny filed for divorce from Lisa in May 2022 after 12 years of marriage. The couple married in 2009 and share two children: son Logan and daughter Elle. In his divorce filing, which was obtained by Page Six, Lenny stated that his “marriage is irretrievably broken and there is no present hope for a meaningful reconciliation.” The filing also notes that Lenny and Lisa signed a prenuptial agreement before they married which requires him to pay spousal support to Lisa.

“I didn’t anticipate this to happen in front of the world. My soon-to-be ex even said to me that he was contemplating waiting until after the show aired to drop this bomb on me,” Lisa told TV Insider in March 2023 about her and Lenny’s split playing out in The Real Housewives of Miami Season 5. “For whatever reason, he decided to drop it. I questioned him asking, ‘Why would you do this?’ It’s super embarrassing. It’s not something I want to show to the world. I wasn’t prepared for that. I was totally blindsided. There are people who have said that I knew this was coming. I absolutely did not know this was coming. He even asked me to have a third child, discussing this among friends at a dinner two weeks before. We would do date nights and movie nights. We were just in Aspen and planning to go to a wedding in Italy with some friends. There are so many things that happened where I thought we were happy. Then boom. He dropped the bomb.”

So what is Lisa Hochstein’s divorce settlement from Lenny? Read more to find out.

What is Lisa Hochstein’s divorce settlement from Lenny?

Lisa and Lenny’s final divorce settlement has not been disclosed, but Lisa has openly talked about the agreements on the show. She moved out of her ex-husband’s house and into an ultra-luxurious, 5,586-square-feet condo with a $32,000-per-month price tag, but is looking for more potential options.

“I love the size of this house,” she said in a January 17, 2024 episode where she toured a $5.387 million house. “This is a wonderful, perfect size for me to raise my family in.”

Lisa admitted that she didn’t know how to pay for the house on her own, “I don’t know because it’s really up to — ultimately Lenny’s decision. This would be part of a settlement.” 

“I do need his support,” she continued. “Because we do have two kids together, and I was married to him for so long.”

In early 2023, the two agreed on a temporary settlement of $8,000 paid towards Lisa, but Lenny fought to withdraw in August 2023. “Lisa came up with an agreement in June. I thought it would have been something that made her happy and allow her to move on because it’s far more generous than our premarital agreement stipulated,” Lenny told Us Weekly at the time. “Lisa will never be happy. I tried my best and instead of having gratitude she doubled down and called me nasty names and accused me of heinous things.”

What is Lisa Hochstein’s Real Housewives of Miami salary?

Lisa Hochstein’s Real Housewives of Miami salary is reportedly around $30,000 per episode. However, according to Lisa, that isn’t sufficient enough for her current lifestyle.

“She has primarily been a homemaker and mother throughout the parties’ marriage,” the divorce filing read, per Page Six. “[Lenny] has significant and substantial assets and earns millions of dollars a year as [a] successful plastic surgeon,” the documents continue, adding that “without [Lenny’s] contribution, [Lisa] cannot provide the needs and necessities of life as they were established during the marriage.”

Lisa asked Lenny to continue to pay for her medical and dental insurance, as well as “non-covered medical, dental and eye care” for her.

What is Lisa Hochstein’s net worth?

Lisa Hochstein’s net worth is estimated to be around $90 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.
