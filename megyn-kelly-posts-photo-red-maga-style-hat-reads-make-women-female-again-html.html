Megyn Kelly posts photo of herself in a red MAGA-style hat that reads: 'Make women female again'

Megyn Kelly has caused a stir after posting a photo of herself wearing a red MAGA-style hat with 'Make Women Female Again' written on it.

The hat was a clear homage to Donald Trump's infamous 'Make America Great Again' hat and her critics were quick to point out that the two had feuded in the past.

The statement on the hat was a pointed reference to ongoing 'culture wars' in the US over trans people and identity.

Kelly, a former Fox News host, has often addressed the issue during The Megyn Kelly Show, which she hosts daily on SiriusXM.

'Thank you for a HUGE week on the⁦ @⁩MegynKellyShow,' Kelly captioned the picture she posted on Twitter on Sunday.

'In Miami celebrating our friend's 50th & looking fwd to another great week of shows starting Monday,' she added.

The most common response to the image were comments regarding the polarizing hat. 

In a later tweet, Kelly linked to the online store she bought it from, called Adult Human Female.

The small brand was created by Kellie-Jay Keen, also known as Posie Parker, a British women's rights activist who started the group Let Women Speak and who has grown to prominence in recent months.

Parker identifies as a TERF, or a trans-exclusionary radical feminist, and has in large text on her website that '2023 is the year of the TERF'. Parker herself is labeled an 'anti-trans' activist by the left.

The controversial TERF movement has been attacked for being 'transphobic' because it refuses to include trans women under the remit of feminism.

Parker appeared on Kelly's show last month in a segment in which the two expressed similar attitudes toward gender and identity.

'I'm a really confident woman, I'm very outspoken,' said Parker when asked by Kelly how she came to create Let Women Speak and champion the cause. 

'I will speak my mind but I've got a daughter who's an introvert, she won't be telling a man in her space to get out and so I just thought I need to step up and if I'm not going to do it then who is,' she added.

Kelly's views on trans issues caused her to become embroiled in her own controversy last month.

She was branded 'transphobic' for calling on the CEO of Anheuser-Busch to apologize for Bud Light's tie-up with trans influencer Dylan Mulvaney, which sparked a considerable backlash from conservatives.

In angry comments on April 17 on The Megyn Kelly Show she accused Mulvaney of making 'a mockery of womanhood and girlhood' and slammed the maker of Bud Light, telling it: 'Screw you and your stupid empty platitudes.'

By promoting a trans woman who is 'celebrated by far-lefties all across the country,' Kelly said Anheuser-Busch had - in their own words - put themselves in the middle 'of a discussion that divides people.'

Kelly's critics accused her of intentionally misgendering Mulvaney and said she came across as transphobic.

But in a tweet later in the week, she hit back, telling her 2.7 million followers on the platform that she was 'speaking the truth'.

'Call me whatever names you want. I am speaking the truth. And neither I nor the millions of other women (and men) who believe in biology/reality/fairness will be dissuaded by your attempts to marginalize us with words like 'transphobic,'' she wrote.
