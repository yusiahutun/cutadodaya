The Y2K poster-girl flaunted her dreamy new bag on Instagram, proving the power of statement accessories

    Nobody does accessories quite like Nicola Peltz. The 28-year-old has perhaps the most enviable collection of handbags on the planet, and she’s just added another Louis Vuitton to her collection that epitomises her status as a Y2K connoisseur.

    The actress is the ultimate icon when it comes to using bags to elevate an outfit. Peltz's typical street-style hack is one we can easily use: wear an unfussy outfit to paint the perfect blank canvas for a statement accessory to shine.

    MORE: Nicola Peltz's white quilted mini bowling bag is perfect for spring: get the look

    READ: Nicola Peltz just recreated an iconic Paris Hilton fashion moment

    Though trends are showing signs of transitioning from Y2K to quiet luxury - a welcomed sight for those who’ve had enough of the throwback trend - Nicola is proving time and time again that the 90s is not going anywhere for spring 2023 – not on her watch (or, bag). We wouldn’t let go either if we could get our hands the not one, but two incredible vintage Louis Vuitton bags that she owns...

    Mrs Peltz-Beckham uploaded an Instagram story for her 2.9 million followers of a mirror selfie that posed no caption, and let her handbag do all the talking. 

    Total vintage was on her sartorial agenda for the day (as with many of her looks). She wore a white cardigan with chunky ribbed detailing, dark blue jeans and a black headband to scrape her fringe back and leave her epic retro Chanel sunglasses in full view.

    MORE: Nicola Peltz & Brooklyn Beckham's date night outfits are going to be your new spring uniform 

    RELATED: Nicola Peltz just wore Victoria Beckham’s favourite 2000s accessory

    The pièce de résistance to her look was the Louis Vuitton Theda that would cause Kim Kardashian, Paris Hilton and Naomi Campbell to have major deja-vu. These three 90s It-girl all owned the bag in some form. 

    Fans of the latest rich girl/luxury trend might consider her new Louis Vuitton ludicrously capaciousbut do they not realise you need a bag big enough to fit a puppy? An obvious requirement for Y2K aficionados...

    Last week, Nicola made another case for large handbags and went full Paris Hilton, wearing a silver Goyard carrying nothing other than a pooch. She also revived the iconic bowling bag that was every It-girl’s dream in the late 90s/2000s (Paris’ plethora of Samantha Thavasa bags were a force to be reckoned with). 

    The ultimate Y2K poster girl.
