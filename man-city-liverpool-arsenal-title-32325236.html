Kyle Walker issues warning to Liverpool and Arsenal as season heads for dramatic climax

Kyle Walker has warned his title rivals Manchester City will fight to the death in a bid to defend their Premier League crown.

Pep Guardiola's champions had to settle for a point following their thrilling 1-1 draw at Liverpool on Sunday. The result saw City drop to third place in the table behind new leaders Arsenal and second place Liverpool.

Arsenal and Liverpool are now a point ahead, with Jurgen Klopp's men appearing to have the easier run in as a remarkable season reaches its climax. But Walker has made it clear City are being driven by the hunger of wanting to win a historic four titles on the spin.

And he admits City will do whatever it takes to remain the champions of England. Walker said: "It's exciting to be involved in. As a player, it's what you want to play in - relishing these opportunities and these rivalries.

"This is why this is the best league in the world. Players want to come here, play and compete in this league.

"We went to Anfield to win and put a stance on the Premier League, but thePremier League is not decided by this result. There's so much football and key, important games to play.

"We go into the big period now after the international break and try to clinch these four Premier Leagues in a row.

"We need to make sure we’re there and fighting until the end, as we always do.

"To win three Premier Leagues in a row is fantastic, but to do four, which no one has ever done, that's what we are striving for - that’s our hunger.

"There’s a lot of football to be played and there’s going to be a lot of twist and turns - as there always is throughout every season.

"We need to make sure they go in our favour and we’re at the top. We’ll keep fighting.”
