A dungeon, secret passageways, peepholes and lingering ghosts: A young couple paid $395,000 in CASH

A young couple just paid $395,000 for a massive castle in the middle of Missouri, but given the low price and unusual circumstances, they may be getting more than they bargained for.

Brandon and Kate Smith are now the proud owners of so-called Stuart Castle, a 7,800-foot home that looks like it belongs on the moors rather than a midwestern suburb.

In spite of the odd location, the castle comes with a number of stereotypical features, like secret passageway, strategically placed peepholes and a dungeon.

Quite the castle: The 7,800-foot Stuart Castle was just bought by a young couple with one son in Missouri

Following through on a promise: A former owner built the castle so that it was fit for his wife who he called 'Princess'

Changing sides: The exterior of the home looks like a castle but it falls back on a normal-looking house

Like many commoners always hoping to live in a castle, the Smiths have longed to own the Stuart castle for a number of years.

The regal-looking home was built by the previous owner, Wallace Stuart, who told the St. Louis Post-Dispatch that he originally purchased the home and the surrounding land in 1968 and promised his wife that it would eventually be fit for a 'princess'.

At one point about ten years ago, he tried to sell the castle for nearly $2million, and while that was out of the Smith's price range, it cemented the listing in their memory.

'I saw pictures of it back then and kind of fell in love with it, but I thought "Oh, that's just a pipe dream,"' Mr Smith, 32, told the paper.

Care: All of the granite used on the property was hand laid

Accents or broad strokes: The stone motif carries throughout the house, like in the kitchen and the living room

Fine touches: The polished wood floors and eight fireplaces give the castle a regal feel

Intricate: There are a few personalized touches in the stonework around the property

Unfortunately for Mr Stuart, he fell on tough economic times and the property has been in bank-owned foreclosure since 2010.

As a result, the price eventually dropped down to $410,000 which piqued the Smith's interest once more.

New occupants: Mortgage broker Brandon Smith, 32, is moving into the house with his wife Kate (not pictured)

The toured their future castle once and decided to buy it and the six acres immediately surrounding it after that first visit.

While the story may sound like that of a fickle financial fairy tale, there are definitely some darker elements lurking on the property.

There is the rumor that the ghost of Mr Stuart's widow still wanders the property, and while that may seem like a falsified horror story, there is some concern that it may be true.

A trespasser was fatally shot on the grounds in 1984, giving the cautionary tales some degree of actual credence.

That said, the Smiths are looking forward to their time in the unusual estate.

The castle comes equipped with four bedrooms, seven bathrooms, a gourmet chefs kitchen featuring an indoor barbeque and eight fireplaces.

According to real estate site Zillow, of the granite used for the exterior and interior floors and walls was hand laid, making it hard to heat but beautiful to admire.

The 'haunted house' atmosphere and requirement that the entire bid be paid in cash mark some similarities between this real story and that of the television show American Horror Story which tells the tale of a family who moves into a large mansion with a mysterious past.

Nevertheless, Mr Smith, a mortgage broker, and his photographer wife, 31, plan to move into their new home with their young son Casen towards the end of the month.
