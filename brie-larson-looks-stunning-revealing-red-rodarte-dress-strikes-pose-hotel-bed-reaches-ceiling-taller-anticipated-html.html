Although the Oscar-winning star is set to reprise her role as Carol Danvers in The Marvels this November, she hasn't been able to promote the film due to the ongoing SAG-AFTRA strike. 

However, based on the trailers for the film, Marvel fans know for certain that Brie will be joined by Teyonah Parris as Monica Rambeau and Iman Vellani as Ms. Marvel respectively. 

In the film Carol has to join forces with Monica and Ms. Marvel to defend the world against Zawe Ashton's villainous Marvels character, Dar-Benn. 

Although the Room actress's first MCU film grossed over a billion dollars, she opened up to Harpers Bazaar (in a March interview conducted before the strike) about how she was initially hesitant to accept the Captain Marvel role. 

'I was scared of what would happen to me,' the Lessons In Chemistry star admitted, not wanting to lose her sense of freedom after joining the MCU. 

She added her concerns about the role inhibiting her artistic process, noting, 'I was like, ‘What world is this, where these are the choices I have to make as an artist?' 

The Fast X star made it clear that she recognized that the stardom with such a role as Captain Marvel could lead to her own delusions of grandeur.

She reflected on that during the interview, saying, 'Anytime I feel like I’m being put too much on a pedestal, it’s my job to figure out how to remove that within myself.'

Marvel aside, the actress is also set to star in Lessons In Chemistry, an Apple TV Plus drama series about a female scientist who hosts her own cooking show. 

The first episode is slated to premiere on October 13th of this year. 

As for the A-lister's persona life, she split with her partner Elijah Allan-Blitz back in March. 

She did find herself wondering what she wanted in terms of her personal life when she hit her 30s, with the Glass Castle star saying, 'What do I want? How does a family and a future fit in with my weird life?'

On turning 33 last year in October, Brie joked, 'Certain existential questions come up.'
