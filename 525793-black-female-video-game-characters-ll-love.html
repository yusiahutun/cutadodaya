15 best black female video game characters you'll love

For the longest time, video games have been filled with mostly white male characters and few female characters of colour. Black characters are especially the least represented, but the numbers have been changing over time. Black female video game characters are diverse and bring in a zesty and fresh perspective. Their strength does not go unnoticed even when some are subjected to unwarranted male gaze and attention.

If you enjoy video games, you know that a great game has a balance between the characters and their roles. Diversity in the gaming world makes it more impressive for players. Like black female anime characters, black female video game characters make the plot spicy.

They come in adorable physical appearances. Everything from their hair, physique, and even costumes stands out from most. These female characters can fight as male characters and can also be vengeful and aggressive as their male counterparts.

Black female video game characters

Like their white male characters, the female characters are skilled in combat, handling weapons and everything about modern warfare. The typical video game player has a few preferences regarding the games they like most.

Whether you are a simulation video gamer or even a casual gamer, you have that character you swear by. There is always that female protagonist or villain character that keeps you spirited whenever you play. Some of the most popular female video game characters, according to Popular Nature include:

1. Sheva Alomar

She is a character on Resident Evil and first appeared in the survival horror video game in 2009. Sheva Alomar came on Resident Evil 5 as a replacement partner for Chris Redfield after the demise of Jill Valentine.

As a member of the Bioterrorism Security Assessment Alliance (BSAA), Sheva possesses an array of fighting skills that are hard to match. She is a sharpshooter and an independent female character.

2. Aveline de Grandpre

Aveline is one of the fan favourite dark-skinned video game characters. This French African assassin will intrigue your interest from the onset. The character was born in the 18th century and lived in New Orleans.

She is different because she does not conform to traditional gender roles. She is a good fighter and not afraid of going to unknown grounds. Aveline de Grandpre is the protagonist you want to watch, especially regarding diverse characters.

3. Alyx Vance

She first appeared in Valve’s Half-Life video game series in 2004 and is known to be an accomplished hacker and a pro at bypassing security systems. Alyx Vance is the kind of character you want to be friends with because she is friendly and full of fun.

She may be battling her demons, especially after the gap her mother’s death left, but she generally has a kind aura and enjoyable personality.

4. Storm

Every video game enthusiast knows Storm as the character who can control the weather. Storm has appeared in multiple video games, including:

  • Marvel’s Midnight Suns
  • Marvel Snap
  • Marvel Future Revolution
  • Marvel Super War
  • Marvel Avengers: Battle for War
  • X-Men

Storm controls the weather by changing the ever-changing energy patterns only visible to her. Apart from weather control, she also has willpower that enables her to fight off telepathic attacks.

5. Vanessa Lewis

She is a character in the Virtua Fighter series. Having grown up as an orphan after the wicked and secretive J6 Corporation killed her parents, Vanessa Lweis did well and made a life for herself despite her misfortunes.

Vanessa’s help came in the form of a man named Lewis, who took and trained her. Unfortunately, J6 got to Lewis and killed him. Vanessa later became a bodyguard and joined the fourth World Fighting Tournament (debuted in VF4)

6. Elena

She is a Kenyan princess in the video game Street Fighter III: New Generation. Elena was taught how to fight the capoeira fighting style from when she was young and perfected her skills as she grew older. She is well-travelled and accommodating.

Elena is the perfect character for those who fancy learning about new people and appreciate our many cultures and traditions. She is one of the best black female fighting game characters.

7. Clementine

If you are looking for a game with black female characters like Clementine, then try The Walking Dead. Clementine is the main protagonist of The Walking Dead video game series. Clem, as she is often referred to, is kindhearted and smart enough to survive on her own.

She appears in the first season, where she is left to fend for herself after the zombie apocalypse. Fans love her because Clem looks past her race and identity and exists on her own. She does not follow the stereotypes; rather, she forges her path.

8. Purna

Before being forced to work as a bodyguard, Purna was a police officer. She got kicked out of the police force for killing a pervert everyone thought was untouchable. The character appears in Dead Island and is one character that loathes every elite who thinks they are above the law.

She does her job well as a VIP and celebrity bodyguard but never lets anyone step on her toes. Purna is good with weapons and just the character you need if you are fascinated by firearms.

9. Lisa Hamilton

Lisa Hamilton first appeared in Dead or Alive Beach Volleyball and made her fighter debut in Dead or Alive 4. She is a former scientist for DOATEC and a brainchild of the Project Alpha project, which spawned Alpha-152, the Kasumi clone.

With everything she has achieved, Lisa is mostly known for her wrestling career. What makes her interesting is her mysterious past and new revelations about her life that resurface every time.

10. Rochelle

Rochelle is the mother figure you did not think you needed until you watched her. The 29-year-old is an inexperienced news reporter in Left 4 Dead 2. Rochelle is from Cleveland, Ohio, and as a go-getter, always determined to reach for more.

She worked as an associate producer for a news company, a role that she felt limited her. Her window of opportunity came after the zombie outbreak, as people called in sick at the office. This gave her a chance to be the news reporter she wanted.

11. D’arci Stern

Wondering about the first black female video game character? It’s D’arci Stern, the lead character in the video game Urban Chaos. Most of her days are spent fighting crime in Union City. D’arci was among the pioneer black female protagonists in a video game.

She is tough and resilient. Her character is not the only thing that makes her stand out, as she has a distinct haircut and style that is noticeably dissimilar from the ordinary female characters in video games.

12. Frey Holland

Alfre Holland, also known as Frey Holland, is a modern lass and the main protagonist of Forspoken. She is a New Yorker transported magically into the land of Athia but now has the urge to go back home. 

Frey faces a couple of challenges; however, to return home, she has to face the Break, a miasma in Athia that jeopardises Athia and its occupants. She also has to face off against the immoral Tanta and defeat them.

13. Nix

Most video game players have a love-hate relationship with Nix. She is from InFamous 2, a citizen of New Marais who opposed the Militia due to Bertrand killing her kin. She has supernatural abilities and can manipulate elements like fire.

Nix is not one to be messed with, as she comes off as cold, callous and intimidating. She is also unpredictable and complex in a way. Those who do not like Nix often side with Kuo, a different character who is less aggressive than Nix.

14. Kendl Johnson

Kendl Johnson’s first appearance is on Grand Theft Auto: San Andres (2004) and later on Grand Theft Auto: The Trilogy - The Definitive Edition (2021). She is CJ and sweet’s sister, the sharpest in the Johnson family.

Kendl Johnson always shows her intelligent side, often shunning the street mentality that her siblings have adopted. She gets better when she enrols in a business school and gets knowledge of property development.

15. Fortune

She is among the best black video game characters. Fortune is from the video game Metal Gear Solid 2. Her real name is Helena Dolph Jackson. The daughter of Scott Dolph, the commandant of the Marine Corps, Fortune lived a pretty ordinary life until her father’s death.

Her father’s death was only the beginning of her misery. A couple of months later, her husband and later mother took their own lives.
