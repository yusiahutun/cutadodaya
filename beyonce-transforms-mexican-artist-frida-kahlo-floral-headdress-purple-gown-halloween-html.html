Beyonce transforms into Mexican artist Frida Kahlo for Halloween

Just hours after stepping out dressed as fellow singer Janet Jackson for Halloween, Beyonce transformed herself into another famous face from a different era altogether.

The hitmaker's colourful headdress was impossible to miss as she channeled Mexican artist Frida Kahlo, whose physical likeness to her has been remarked on in the past.

The 33-year-old made a beeline for New York's Charlie Bird, while accompanied by daughter Blue Ivy and husband Jay Z to watch the city's parade.

The Two Fridas: The hitmaker sported a colourful headdress and chunky gold earrings (L), which the Mexican artist is famous for wearing (R)

Beyonce stood out from the crowd thanks to her eye-catching hair accessories, featuring pink blooms and vibrant butterflies.

Wearing flowers in her hair and chunky jewelry is something the artist - who died in 1954 - often did, especially for her iconic self-portraits.

The Crazy In Love star's gold earrings were worn with a floral print gown, complete with spotted neck tie, and sheer sleeves. 

Mini-me: The 33-year-old was accompanied by her adorable daughter Blue Ivy, who was dressed in a comic-print blazer

Coupled up: The mother-of-one was also pictured with husband Jay Z for a front row view of the NYC parade

She was seen crouching down to talking to her two-year-old, who was wearing a fun, comic-print blazer.

Meanwhile Jay Z also appeared to take inspiration from a famous artist for his costume, in the form of Jean-Michel Basquiat.

The rapper's normally short tresses were hidden under an voluminous black wig, styled into four vertical spikes.

Keeping close: The 99 Problems rapper appeared to copy artist Jean-Michel Basquiat's hair 'do for the night (L) while Beyonce chose a vibrant purple dress with floral pattern

Hair-raising: The 44-year-old's normally short tresses were hidden under a spiked-up wig

Own it: The star is known to be a fan of Jean-Michel (pictured left in 1985 with Andy Warhol) after it was reported last year that he had snapped up his Mecca painting for a cool $4.5 million

The look was made famous by the Brooklyn-born star, who achieved notoriety as part of informal graffiti group SAMO, before dying of a heroin overdose in 1988.

The 99 Problems star is known to be a fan of Jean-Michel after it was reported last year that he had snapped up his Mecca painting for a cool $4.5 million.

Jay Z also remarked that one of his works, Charles the First, had inspired his 2010 song Most Kingz, the New York Post stated.
