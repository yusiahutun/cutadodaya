Biography

John Cazale was born in Revere, MA on August 12, 1935. Remembered for his roles in such celebrated films as The Godfather, The Conversation, Dog Day Afternoon, and The Deer Hunter, this acclaimed actor was known for his multi-layered portrayals of violent and unstable, yet vulnerable, characters. From 1976 until his death from lung cancer in 1978, he was romantically involved with the acclaimed film actress Meryl Streep. He met his future artistic collaborator, Al Pacino while working for the New York City office of Standard Oil.
