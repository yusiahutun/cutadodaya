Medicine, 9 Other Lucrative Jobs in Kenya that Pay Over KSh 150k Monthly

  • Kenyan judges also are renumerated well due to their huge responsibilities in providing justice to the people

There are 19.1 million Kenyans in the labour market according to the 2023 economic survey report released by the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS).

In 2022, the economy created 816,000 new jobs despite the harsh conditions which included prolonged drought, rising inflation and political uncertainties.

The report indicated that during the period under review, jobs created in the private sector decreased from 125,000 reported in 2021 to 94,500.

KNBS noted non-governmental organisations (NGOs) top the list of the highest-paying sectors.

The report revealed wage employment in the private sector increased by 4.8% in 2022.

"Total employment outside small-scale agriculture and pastoral activities was 19.1 million in 2022 up from 18.3 million recorded in 2021. Employment in the informal sector rose by 4.6% to 16.0 million jobs. Wage employment in the private sector recorded a growth of 4.8% from 1,983.0 thousand jobs in 2021 to 2,077.5 thousand jobs in 2022. Within the public sector, wage employment increased from 923.1 thousand jobs in 2021 to 937.9 thousand jobs in 2022," the survey revealed.

Highest paying careers in Kenya

According to Salary Explorer, a salary comparison and career resources website for employees and employers, surgeons and doctors top the list of the highest-paying careers in Kenya.

1. Surgeons/doctors' salary in Kenya

Surgeons/doctors take home an average of KSh 546,000 monthly.

Their salaries range from KSh 284,000 to KSh 846,000 per month.

"A surgeon's profession involves high risk and requires extensive knowledge and a long learning path; the necessary ingredients for a high-paying career," Salary Explorer stated.

2. Judges' salary in Kenya

Kenyan judges are renumerated well due to their huge responsibilities in providing justice to the people.

The Judiciary notes that judges are mandated to administer justice in a fair, timely, accountable and accessible manner.

They are required to uphold the rule of law, advance indigenous jurisprudence and protect the Constitution.

Judges are paid an average of KSh 459,000 monthly with their salaries ranging from KSh 239,000 to KSh 702,000.

3. Kenya lawyers' salary

Law is one of the highest-paying jobs in Kenya, ranking third behind doctors and judges.

Lawyers are trained to help society in conflict management through various mechanisms, including litigation, negotiation, mediation, arbitration and adjudication.

4. Kenyan bank managers' pay

Kenya has over 1400 bank branches according to the Central Bank of Kenya's annual banking sector supervision report.

5. Chief executive officers (CEOs)

CEOs run the companies on a daily basis and are responsible for success or failure.

Their monthly salaries range from KSh 170,000 to KSh 501,000.

In related news, the findings of the highest-paying jobs per sector in Kenya are contained in the 2023 economic survey.
