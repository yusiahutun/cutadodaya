&quot;We'll Continue Praising God&quot;: SDA Choir Unleashing Killer Dance Moves on Streets Shuts Down Critics

  • The Bridal Choir faced huge backlash over the weekend following their electrifying matching dance which many faithful termed ungodly
  • The singers have hit back at the critics, saying they won't stop dancing and praising God in their own way
  • They supported their matching style with a Bible verse which allows humans to praise God with joyous music

The Bridal Choir of the Seventh Day Adventist Church (SDA) has reacted to the claims that their funny matching moves, which recently went viral are exaggerated and ungodly.

Bridal Choir moves "not SDA-like"

A recent video of the singers unleashing riveting matching moves in the streets of Voi Town elicited reactions.

Performing one of their viral songs Salama, the choir, dressed in crisp and well-pressed checked dresses for women and checked half coats for men, put their best feet forward and it was lovely.

Aligned in four columns, the singing group was guided by two energetic men with athletic bodies who provided the right tempo and rhythm for the match.

The video sparked reaction as a section of staunch SDA members criticised their matching style, terming it ungodly.

Bridal choir vows not to stop dancing

In a Facebook post sighted by TUKO.co.ke, the singers told naysayers that they won't stop praising God in the way they best know.

They supported their unique moves with scripture from Psalms 149:3 which says:

"Praise his name with dancing: play drums and harps in praise of him."

The choir wondered how they should put the verse into practice if their matching style was considered ungodly.

"By the way, the book of psalms 149: 3 is to be applied how? Or we shouldn't even read it even if it's in the Bible? We ask in peace," read the Facebook post.

It should be noted that SDA churches don't encourage dancing while praising God and this has seen many SDAs ganging up against the choir and labelling their moves "ungodly".

It is, however, a fact that SDA church is divided on if such moves are Godly or not.

Most English-speaking or urban SDA churches frown at such moves, describing them as satanic or secular.

On the other hand, such moves are popular in Kiswahili-speaking and rural SDA churches.

Apart from dancing, the SDA church also frowns at clapping, shouting in church, repetitive prayer, and prayer labyrinths.

This is how those irked by the choir responded:

Benjah Osinde: "This is satanic."

Bridal Choir: "Benjah Osinde As a staunch believer, you should have the power to cast out the demons/satan, please do!"

Davies G Videz: "What did I just see? Shame on them."

Bridal Choir: "Davies G Videz Na bado! Be ready to repeat this same word here severally!"

Don't bully us

The singers of God said they won't allow naysayers to discourage them from praising God.

"We shall not allow you to bully us and expect us to quit! We shall go to every length of this journey with you."
