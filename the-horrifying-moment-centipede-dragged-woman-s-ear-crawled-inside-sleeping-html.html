CENTIPEDE pulled from woman's ear canal after crawling inside while she was asleep

This stomach-churning video shows the hideous moment a centipede is pulled from a woman's ear.

The creature had crawled into the unwitting Chinese woman's ear as she was sleeping.

Upon waking, and feeling something definitely wasn't right, she rushed to the emergency room of Yangzhou hospital in the eastern province of Jiangsu, China.

There, doctors discovered the bug in her ear and were able to kill it using a saline solution.

They then used an electric otoscope - a medical device used to look into the ears - to find it, and pull it out.

The otoscope had a camera attached and so a video of the whole process became available.

The footage shows doctors trying to clamp the centipede's body with tweezers, but finding it difficult not to rip part of the creature's body off and leave the rest inside.

They then drag the lifeless invertebrate's body, which is brown and yellow coloured, out of the woman's ear.

Small centipedes produce a painful reaction similar to a bee sting, experts warn.

However, larger ones have more venom and bites from them can be extremely painful.

The woman is said to be recovering well but did suffer minor injuries to her ear. 

The unnamed woman rushed to the emergency room of Yangzhou Hospital in the eastern province of Jiangsu. Doctors killed bug with a saline solution and dragged it out of the woman's ear using tweezers

The news comes after MailOnline reported on the story of a teenager who pulled a live centipede from his ear in July this year.

Grant Botti,14, said he woke up in excruciating pain.

He reached into his ear and pulled out the culprit: a four-inch long centipede. 

Mr Botti's mother, Angela, placed the centipede, still alive, in a plastic bag and took the teen, of Saline County, to the emergency room, KATV reports.

A doctor found small abrasions in Mr Botti's ear drum. He was treated and discharged.

Mrs Botti told KATV they didn't know where the bug had come from, but her son had recently been swimming outdoors.
