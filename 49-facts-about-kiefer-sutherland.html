Early Beginnings

Kiefer Sutherland was born on December 21, 1966, in London, England. His parents, Donald Sutherland and Shirley Douglas, were both esteemed actors.

Dual Citizenship

Although born in England, Kiefer Sutherland also holds Canadian citizenship due to his parents’ nationality.

Family Ties

Kiefer comes from a talented family of actors. In addition to his parents, his maternal grandfather, Tommy Douglas, was a prominent Canadian politician and his twin sister, Rachel Sutherland, is also an actress.

Acting Debut

Kiefer made his acting debut at the age of 17 in the 1983 film Max Dugan Returns, alongside his father.

Breakthrough Role

One of Kiefer’s most iconic roles came in 1986 when he portrayed the rebellious country boy, Ace Merrill, in the film adaptation of Stephen King’s Stand by Me.

Jack Bauer in “24”

Kiefer gained widespread recognition for his role as Jack Bauer in the television series 24, which aired from 2001 to His intense portrayal of the counter-terrorism agent garnered critical acclaim.

Emmy Award Winner

For his role in 24, Kiefer won the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series in 2006.

A Musically Inclined Actor

In addition to acting, Kiefer is also a talented musician. He released his debut country music album, Down in a Hole, in 2016.

Voice Acting in Video Games

Kiefer lent his voice to the character of Snake in the popular video game series Metal Gear Solid V: The Phantom Pain in 2015.

Kiefer’s Role in “Young Guns”

Kiefer played the notorious gunslinger, Doc Scurlock, in the 1988 Western film Young Guns.

Hollywood Walk of Fame

In 2008, Kiefer was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame for his contributions to the entertainment industry.

A Talented Director

Besides acting, Kiefer has also proven his skills behind the camera. He directed several episodes of the television series 24.

Connection to “Flatliners”

Kiefer starred alongside Julia Roberts and Kevin Bacon in the 1990 psychological horror film Flatliners, which he later reprised in the 2017 sequel.

Kiefer’s Role in “A Few Good Men”

Kiefer portrayed Lieutenant Jonathan Kendrick in the 1992 legal drama film A Few Good Men, sharing the screen with Tom Cruise and Jack Nicholson.

Kiefer’s Height

Kiefer stands at a height of 5 feet 9 inches (1.75 meters).

Collaboration with Joel Schumacher

Kiefer worked with director Joel Schumacher on two films: The Lost Boys (1987) and Phone Booth (2002).

Role in “Melancholia”

Kiefer played the character John, the wedding planner, in the 2011 Lars von Trier film Melancholia.

Kiefer’s Interest in Rodeo

Outside of his career in the entertainment industry, Kiefer has a passion for rodeo and even participated in the Professional Rodeo Cowboys Association circuit.

Kiefer’s Role in “Dark City”

In the 1998 science fiction film Dark City, Kiefer starred as Dr. Daniel P. Schreber, a pivotal character in the surreal dystopian world.

Canadian Screen Award

In 2016, Kiefer received a Canadian Screen Award for Best Performance by an Actor in a Leading Role for the film Forsaken.

Kiefer’s Role in “The Lost Boys”

Kiefer played the charismatic vampire, David, in the cult classic film The Lost Boys (1987), which became a defining role for him early in his career.

Collaboration with Julia Roberts

Kiefer shared the screen with Julia Roberts in the films Flatliners (1990), Love Streams (1984), and Article 99 (1992).

Kiefer’s Role in “Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me”

Kiefer had a cameo appearance as samurai Jean Renault in the 1992 film Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me, based on the television series Twin Peaks.

Voice of General W.R. Monger

Kiefer provided the voice for General Warren R. Monger in the animated film Monsters vs. Aliens (2009).

Dedication to Charity Work

Kiefer is actively involved in various charitable causes, including Amnesty International, Artists for Peace and Justice, and the AIDS Foundation.

Role as Young Snake Plissken

Kiefer played the younger version of the character Snake Plissken, originally portrayed by Kurt Russell, in the 1996 film Escape from L.A..

Collaboration with Robert Downey Jr.

Kiefer and Robert Downey Jr. shared the screen in the 1988 film 1969, exploring the impact of the Vietnam War on their characters.

Kiefer’s Role in “The Three Musketeers”

Kiefer as David in “The Sentinel”

Kiefer’s Role in “Mirrors”

Kiefer’s Role in “Eye for an Eye”
