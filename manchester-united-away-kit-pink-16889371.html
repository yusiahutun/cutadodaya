Manchester United away kit: New pink shirt unveiled by Adidas for 2018/19

The Red Devils have released their first ever pink shirt, made by Adidas.

The colour has been chosen as a tribute to the Manchester Evening News ‘Football Pink’ publication.

‘Football Pink’ was an extra newspaper released by the Manchester Evening News on Saturday afternoons, but it was closed down in 2007.

United confirmed the shirt would be pink on the cover of their match programme for last night’s 3-0 defeat to Tottenham, and today the shirt is available to a small number of fans at the Adidas store on Manchester’s Market Street.

It will also be available at the same venue tomorrow, before it becomes available at all Adidas and Manchester United official stores on Thursday 13 September.

Adidas say the shirt features “a new reworked performance silhouette, the design combines a colour firmly part of United’s rich football heritage with a contemporary pastel trend to create a shirt that is designed for ultimate wearability and style.”

United usually tend to rotate between blue, white and black away and third kits.

This season their third kit is dark blue, while last season they had a black away kit.

They have occasionally dabbled with grey kits - with the last campaign’s third kit the first time the colour had been used since a famous shirt in the 1990s that once had to be changed at half time when the players complained they could not pick out their team-mates against the backdrop of the crowd.

Gold and green kits have also appeared in the past, but this is the first time pink has been selected as the away colour.
