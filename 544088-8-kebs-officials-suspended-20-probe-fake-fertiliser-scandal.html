8 KEBS Officials Suspended, 20 Others under Probe over Fake Fertilizer Scandal

  • Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) officials have been suspended, while 20 others are under investigation over their alleged involvement in the fake fertilizer scandal
  • KEBS stated that all subsidized fertilizers meet the required standards except those from KEL Chemicals Limited
  • Agriculture Cabinet Secretary Mithika Linturi had denied the presence of fake fertilizer in the market, accusing the opposition of spreading false information

Eight government officials working for the Kenya Bureau of Statistics (KEBS) have been suspended, while 20 others are under probe over the fake fertilizer scandal.

The officials allegedly gave the green light to two companies to use the KEBS diamond mark to give the fake fertilizers authenticity and pass them as genuine products.

The companies linked to the distribution of fake fertilizers include KEL Chemicals Limited, whose products were suspended from the market after KEBS confirmed that they did not meet the required standards.

According to a press statement released by the ministry, most of the subsidized fertilizer in the market was up to standard except the products from KEL Chemicals branded Kelphos Plus, Kelphos Gold and NPK 10:26:10.

"The government has completed the testing on all fertilizers being distributed under subsidy programme and wish to confirm to the public and farmers that all fertilizers being distributed meet the required quality requirements except those manufactured and distributed by Ms KEL Chemicals branded Kelphos Plus, Kelphos gold and NPK 10:26:10, which did not meet all required test parameters," the statement read in part.

Boss Shollei's Take

In response to the suspension of KEBS officials, National Assembly Deputy Speaker Glady Boss Shollei emphasized that the officials involved should be dismissed.

Speaking during an interview with Citizen TV, Shollei asserted that KEBS should take responsibility and ensure suspended officials do not receive their salaries.

"We need to get our officials to take personal responsibility because whatever else comes into the country, it will be KEBS who will check, "she stated.

President William Ruto also expressed his frustration with the matter and has vowed to deal with those trying to undermine the country's food production program by selling fake fertilizer.

Govt admits the existence of fake fertilizer in the market

On Friday, April 5, the government, through the Ministry of Agriculture, admitted the presence of fake fertiliser in the market.

Linturi urged Kenyans to ignore reports about fake fertilizer.

He accused the opposition of spreading false information to sabotage President Ruto's agenda.
