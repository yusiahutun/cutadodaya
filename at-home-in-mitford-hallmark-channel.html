'At Home in Mitford' Coming to Hallmark Channel

When At Home in Mitford debuted in 1994, it launched a bestselling book series and a new career for first-time author and North Carolina native Jan Karon. Now, more than 20 years later, it's getting the movie treatment it deserves, thanks to the Hallmark Channel and star-slash-co-executive producer Andie MacDowell.

The story follows children's author Cynthia Coppersmith (MacDowell), a newcomer to Mitford, North Carolina, who's staying at her late uncle's cottage in hopes of overcoming writer's block. As she gets to know her next-door neighbor, the Episcopal priest Father Tim (Cameron Mathison), the young boy he's been given guardianship over, and other members of the community and local church, Coppersmith finds the extended family that's been missing from her busy life.

Getty Images

MacDowell, who grew up in Gaffney, South Carolina, and spent summers in Arden, North Carolina (her family's former summer home is now a charming historical inn), says there's "something sweet about living in a small town." That, and the fact that so many of her friends and family members had read and loved the Mitford books, attracted her to the project. She first approached producer Howard Braunstein, who originally optioned the rights from Jan Karon, several years ago while starring in the small-town drama Cedar Cove, also on Hallmark.

"I wish I could find Father Tim. I think that's part of the appeal to me: I haven't really found Father Tim in my life, so I get to live it in fantasy."

"The movie is about how fulfilling it is to be a person that takes care of other people. You get more by caring for others—you're the one that ends up receiving," MacDowell tells CountryLiving.com.

"By watching Father Tim and learning from him and how he lives his life, all the kindness that is in him, Cynthia sees she was missing that in her fast-paced world," she adds.

Courtesy of Hallmark Channel

With motherhood taking center stage as the most-fulfilling role in her own life—"I know how lucky I am to have my three children"—MacDowell says she empathized with her character, a woman who wanted children but moved past her childbearing years without ever having any. "She's a mature woman who finally gets this opportunity to feel what it would be like to be a mother...provided to her through the situation, by moving in next door to Father Tim and having this young child come into her life."

Courtesy of Hallmark Channel

Playing Cynthia wasn't a too far of a stretch for MacDowell, who is twice divorced and living in southern California with her Boston Terrier/Chihuahua mix Ava, whom she adopted from the Lange Foundation. "I was able to use a lot of my real self for this character," she says. "I wish I could find Father Tim. I think that's part of the appeal to me: I haven't really found Father Tim in my life, so I get to live it in fantasy."

There's an underlying message of faith and spirituality too, of course, as Father Tim is the central character in the books. Cynthia isn't much of a churchgoer at first, but as her connections in Mitford deepens, she learns "how deeply she could be fed by that atmosphere," says MacDowell. "We are all in need of belonging and having healthy connections and a place where we feel safe."

Hallmark Channel's At Home in Mitford airs Sunday, Aug. 20 at 10/9 central.
