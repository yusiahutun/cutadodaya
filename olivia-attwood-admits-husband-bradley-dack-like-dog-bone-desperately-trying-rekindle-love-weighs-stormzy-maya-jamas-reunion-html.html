Olivia Attwood admits now-husband Bradley Dack was 'like a dog with a bone' while desperately trying

Olivia Attwood has shed light on how she reunited with her then-ex Bradley Dack after leaving Love Island - after which the couple tied the knot. 

Appearing on Loose Women on Monday, the star chatted on the panel about the showbiz news-dominating confirmation of Maya Jama and Stormzy's romance, four years after they parted ways, as they holidayed together in Greece. 

The panel spoke to Olivia, who married footballer Bradley earlier this year, about whether it is worth waiting for love - leading her to regale her own story. 

The stunner explained that she split with Bradley to go into the villa in 2017, during which she was coupled with Chris Hughes however when she left the show Bradley began pleading for a reunion, with Olivia saying: 'He was like a dog with a bone'. 

She said: 'He messaged me like two days after I came out of the villa off someone else's phone and was like 'We're not done, we'll never be done' - so dramatic - and I was like 'This guy, honestly, is like a dog with a bone'.

When she was asked if she thought he could be The One, she said: 'No because I ended the relationship to go on Love Island for good reasons. Like I thought no, I'm not getting involved in that kind of relationship...

'I want something more serious and all that stuff, but it turns out if I hadn't gone on the show and we hadn't had that break we 100% would not be together now and married, no way'.

Olivia and Chris' relationship was famously tumultuous, with Olivia telling MailOnline after their split that her relationship with Chris was so 'all consuming' and 'toxic' that it pushed her back into Bradley's arms.

She said: 'Me and Brad were just lying in bed the other day and we thought how mad our journey has been. Obviously we had the breakup and everything was in the air.

'I thought when I was in Love Island that was it. I was so done with him but then I ended up in another relationship that ended up being so toxic and all-consuming.

'And sometimes that's what happens and it pushes you back to your ex. After that we needed a good year to find our footing as a couple again. I still wasn't sure.

'It was toxic with Chris and we argued a lot. I never had that problem with Brad, we never argued.'

She continued: 'I was just incompatible with Chris. You meet on this show and things are very different in the real world. You think 'oh s**t do we actually have stuff in common?' It wasn't meant to be.'

Chris meanwhile said of their romance and split: 'We had great times together. But not everything lasts. That’s the beauty of life. There is no script...

'I don’t regret anything. Her and Brad are meant to be. That’s the way life is. I’ll get married one day, hopefully, maybe. I don’t want to grow up just yet.'
