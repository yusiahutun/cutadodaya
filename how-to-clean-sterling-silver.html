How to clean silver jewellery

It’s a scene many of us are familiar with – digging through the depths of your jewellery box, you find a silver piece that you love but haven’t worn in a while as it’s looking lacklustre. All precious metals are prone to scratches and dirt build up, but it's important to make sure that you’re cleaning each metal in the right way to avoid any permanent damage.

With gold, you will need to be careful with the different karat weights, and the gems set within it, before polishing, while earrings are extremely fiddly so can cause problems of their own. With sterling silver, you need a multi-faceted approach.

Below, we round up top tips from jewellery experts on how to clean your favourite silver jewellery, and keep it sparkling and scratch-free.

Avoid harsh chemicals and cosmetics

“Silver requires a little more care and maintenance than gold. It is prone to tarnishing so regular cleaning and polishing is needed to keep it close to its original condition,” says Jack Cannon, co-founder of London-based jewellery brand Hatton Labs. “The best way to keep your silver looking its best is to avoid exposure to chemicals; remove your jewellery before using detergents or cleaning products and be cautious with lotions, perfumes and hairspray as these are the slow killers of silver,” he explains. It’s a good idea to take your silver off before exercising too, as sweat can accelerate the tarnishing process.

Prevent tarnishing with silver polish

“It feels counterintuitive, but wearing your pieces regularly can actually help prevent tarnish build up,” explains Anna Jewsbury, founder of London-based brand Completed Works, who regularly works in silver set with crystals. “If tarnishing does occur, specialist polishing cloths are great for sterling silver but can remove the plating of silver-plated pieces over time, so a better option is a plain microfibre cloth.” There are natural irritants to silver too, such as salt air and salt water, which can both reduce its bright white shine. “After exposure to salty water, natural lustre and original brightness can be restored in just a few minutes with an off-the-shelf silver polishing product available in most hardware stores,” advises Louie Cresswell, founder of Ouie, who crafts sleek sterling silver toggle and keyring-inspired pieces.

Consider storage carefully

Because silver scores lower than gold alloys on the MOH scale (the scale that determines hardness and durability) it is more prone to scratching. “Most small scratches can be polished out with a polishing cloth, but deeper scratches will need to be buffed out by a professional,” explains Cresswell. The best approach is prevention rather than cure, and this is where proper storage makes all the difference. “The best way to store silver is an airtight container, ideally with dividers to separate each piece so that scratches don’t occur,” says Jewsbury. “Try to avoid extreme temperatures and humidity if you can – if you live in a humid climate, I recommend placing a small packet of desiccant crystals in your jewellery box.”

Take your lead from the professionals

Some brands, such as London-based label Otiumberg, provide specialist pouches to store your pieces in, which takes some of the worry away. You can also buy an ultrasonic cleaner for home use – the machine many jewellers use for professional cleaning, which uses high frequency pressure waves to dislodge dirt. It is, however, important to not clean pieces set with porous pearls, emeralds, or opals this way, as it can damage the gems. The golden rule? “When in doubt,” says Canon, “it's best to consult with a professional.”
