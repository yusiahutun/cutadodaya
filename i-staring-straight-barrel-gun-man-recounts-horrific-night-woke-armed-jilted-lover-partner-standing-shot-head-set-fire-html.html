Paul Gray remembers his partner's ex shoot him in the head and set him on fire

The man who miraculously survived being shot in the head and set alight, has spoken about the terrifying near-death experience when he was caught up in a deadly love triangle.

Solicitor Paul Gray went on his first date with Claire Carey, 44, before the pair headed back to her place just hours before they were gunned down by her ex-boyfriend Michael Patrick Staines, 46.

Just weeks before the fatal shooting, which rocked the community of Townsville in north Queensland eight years ago, Ms Carey had ended her relationship with Staines after finding out he was married.

But days leading up to her first night out with her new lover, who she had met at work, Staines was introduced to Mr Gray when he popped in for a visit, which soon led to the pair being stalked.

Appearing on Channel Nine's Inside Story, Mr Gray, who is now 52-years-old, recalls the harrowing ordeal when he woke up to find Staines standing at the end of the bed armed with a rifle.

'I was staring straight down the barrel of the gun,' Mr Gray told Inside Story, which aired on Sunday night.

Staines had let himself into Ms Carey's home with a spare key she had given him before bursting into the bedroom to find the pair in bed together at about 1am on November 19, 2006.

'Pure anger - rage - he was angry,' he said of Staines' behaviour that night.

Ms Carey tried to calm Staines down as he screamed obscenities before he fatally shot her twice in the chest.

'She had a wound in the chest that was sort of bubbling out [sic]. She was covered in blood - her hair was spread out all over the pillows so I knew she was in big trouble,' he recalls.

Ms Carey (left) had ended her relationship with Michael Staines (right) after finding out he was still married

Staines fatally shot Ms Carey but was later found dead in an apparent murdered-suicide with a gunshot wound

Staines then turned the gun on her new boyfriend and chillingly mimics the same words spoken just days before the men were introduced by Ms Carey.

'He said "Pleased to meet you Mick" and pulled the trigger,' Mr Gray said.

'I saw the sparks come out at the end of the gun. I was losing consciousness and the last thoughts that I had were it's been a great life.

Mr Gray suffered a gunshot wound to the head but miraculously, the bullet didn't penetrate his skull.

'He [Staines] said wait till you see what's coming next and I didn't know whether that meant a bigger gun or if he was going to drag a canon into the room or what it was,' Mr Gray said.

Staines doused fuel around the house and set it alight before escaping but was later found dead in an apparent suicide with a gunshot wound to the head outside an ice-making business.

Mr Gray tried desperately to escape the fire but feared Staines would shoot him as he relentlessly waited outside the window of the burning building.

'I looked down there and saw this Mick character again pointing the gun at me,' he said.

As he jumped through the window and started running down the street to seek help, Staines followed behind in his car while firing his gun at him.

Mr Gray managed to run to safety at a neighbour's home before he was rushed to hospital to have the bullet removed from his skull. He also suffered serious burns to the lower half of his body.
