The talking Fisher Price doll that 'preaches pro-Islamic message to toddlers'

By Daily Mail Reporter
Updated: 04:28 EST, 14 October 2008

View
comments


Hateful doll? Some parents are convinced that the Little Mommy Cuddle 'n Coo doll 'tells children to follow Islam'

A Fisher Price doll has been accused of preaching pro-Islamic messages to toddlers.

In a bizarre - and barely believable outburst -  the Little Mommy Cuddle 'n Coo toy is accused of proclaiming 'Islam is the light' to baffled children.

It is also reported to have uttered: 'Satan is King'.

The messages are said to have come from the mouth of a doll whose vocabulary is restricted to coos and giggles, which may seem somewhat surprising.

But concerned parents have now reported the American plaything, which is also available in the UK at £19.99, to the makers.

Some retailers responded by removing the product from the shelves.

The story emerged on Fox News, a channel which has attracted more than its fair share of controversy.

Mattel, the parent company of Fisher Price, has dismissed the claims as a phenomenon caused by the doll's cheap inbuilt speakers.

A spokesman said: 'The Little Mommy Cuddle 'n Coo dolls feature realistic baby sounds including cooing, giggling, and baby babble with no real sentence structure.

They refute allegations that a Muslim worker has tampered with the voice box.

The spokesman said: 'The only scripted word the doll says is 'mama'. There is a sound that may resemble something close to the word 'night', 'right', or 'light'.

'Because the original soundtrack is compressed into a file that can be played through an inexpensive toy speaker, actual sounds may be imprecise or distorted.'

Audio expert John Gaudet studied the recording and concluded it sounded most like 'It's not near the light'.

'People want it to say something that it's actually not; the ears can be deceiving,' Mr Gaudet said.

This isn't the first time talking dolls have caused problems.

Doll controversy: One parent claimed this year that a Tickle Me Elmo doll made death threats against her son

In 1998 parents across the U.S. were angry because they believed their children's Teletubby dolls were saying 'Faggot, faggot, bite my butt'.

Two years later, a Californian mother threatened a lawsuit because she was convinced that a Teletubby doll was telling her child 'I got a gun, I got a gun, run away, run away'.

In 2006 a mother complained that a Mattel Little Mermaid doll called her daughter 'a slut'.

Stephanie Herrera of San Jose said she discovered the fluke when her three-year-old daughter, Juliana, was pressing the button quickly so she could skip the phrases and get to the melody.

Earlier this year Florida parents claimed that a Tickle Me Elmo doll, another Mattel product, made death threats against their son, James.

Mother Melissa Bowman said that with a squeeze of its fuzzy belly, the Sesame Street character said: 'Kill James.'
