Bond girl Lea Seydoux debuts new light blonde pixie cut at The French Dispatch screening in Paris

She's the much-loved French actress who played opposite Daniel Craig's James Bond in the latest instalments of the franchise.

And, Lea Seydoux debuted a new light blonde pixie cut as she stepped out in a modest black-and-white ensemble at The French Dispatch gala screening at Cinema UGC Normandie in Paris, France on Sunday. 

The Bond girl, 36, looked chic in her knee-length sleeveless dress, which she wore over a striped collared shirt.

Gorgeous Lea Seydoux, 36, debuted a light blonde pixie cut while in a modest black-and-white ensemble at The French Dispatch screening at Cinema UGC Normandie in Paris on Sunday

Lea drew attention to her slim waist with the help of a chunky black belt and boosted her height with shiny black heeled thigh-high boots. 

The French actress accessorised with statement diamond stud earrings but all attention was on her shorter, and much blonder locks.

The actress was last seen with a short brunette hairstyle at the No Time To Die premiere in London, but has switched things up with new platinum tresses.

The French Dispatch, which is directed by Wes Anderson, is set in a fictional French city and follows journalists who work in an American publication called The French Dispatch Magazine. 

The flick brings to life a collection of stories in the magazine, and follows the many different characters which are played by a stand-out cast. 

Lea Seydoux takes on the role of a prison guard looking after a troubled artist (played by Benicio Del Toro).  

Gorgeous: Lea enhanced her natural beauty with a light dusting of make-up

(Pictured L_R with Alexandre Desplat, Cecile De France, Wes Anderson and Lyna Khoudri)

Lea and Benicio star alongside Bill Murray, Adrien Brody, Tilda Swinton, Frances McDormand, Timothée Chalamet, and Owen Wilson - amongst others.

Seydoux, whose French mother tongue is put to great use during her appearance, plays the only character who speaks the language in the film. 

Set to be released in the UK on October 22nd 2021, the movie started filming back in November 2018 and wrapped in March 2019 but has delayed release due to the pandemic.
