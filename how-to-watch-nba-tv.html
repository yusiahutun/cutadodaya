If you’re constantly flipping the channel to a basketball game, you probably want to know how to watch NBA TV. The network offers everything you’d want to see from the NBA—including live games, featured shows, replays of previous games, and more.

NBA TV, launched on November 2, 1999, is an American sports-oriented pay television network that’s owned by the National Basketball Association (NBA) and run by Warner Bros. Discovery through TNT Sports. It broadcasts NBA exhibition, regular season, and playoff games, along with analysis programs, specials, and documentaries. NBA was the first professional sports league to start a subscription network, followed by the NFL (NFL Network) and NHL (NHL Network).

What can you watch on NBA TV? NBA TV will air 130 exclusive games live (regional sports networks will also air the games) during the 2023-2024 season. Additionally, you’ll find a lot of analysis and commentary. According to DirecTV, NBA Gametime Live, NBA TV Marquee Matchup, Best of Crunchtime, and High Tops are among the most popular programs NBA TV offers. The network also features NBA TV originals—like series, documentaries, and interviews—some of which include Basketball Stories, Read or Not (Parts 1 & 2), Kobe Bryant: Throughout the Years, Allen Iverson: The Answer, and Everything But the Chip: The 2001 76ers.

These are just a few examples of what you can find on NBA TV, so keep reading to discover how to watch NBA TV to learn about its other programs.

How to watch NBA TV live for free

NBA TV is available to stream on services like DirecTV Stream, Fubo, and Sling. DirecTV Stream offers a five-day free trial, which starts at $79.99 per month after the trial ends. Fubo offers a seven-day free trial, which starts at $74.99 per month after the trial ends. To watch NBA TV, you’ll need the Fubo Extra add-on, which is an additional $7.99 per month. Sling offers three plans—Sling Orange, Sling Blue, and Sling Orange + Blue—starting at $20 for your first month. Sling Orange includes ESPN and TNT, but to watch NBA TV, fans will need the service’s Sports Extra package. Sports Extra costs $11 per month when added to Sling Orange or Sling Blue or $15 per month if added to Sling Orange + Sling Blue subscription.

DirecTV is our best overall pick to watch NBA TV live for free for its free trial, price, and channel selection. DirecTV Stream offers a five-day free trial and has a current promo where you can sign up for DirecTV and get up to a $200 VISA Reward Card. You can also save $50 over two months when you purchase the Choice or Ultimate package, which typically costs $108.99 per month but is down to $83.99 per month with the promo. All plans include NBA TV, except for Entertainment.

As for other differences between the plans, Entertainment includes more than 75 channels, the ability to stream on unlimited devices in your home, unlimited cloud DVR storage, and a special offer on premium channels. Ultimate includes more than 140 channels and everything included in Entertainment and Choice. Premiere includes more than 150 channels including premium channels like HBO and Starz and everything included in Entertainment, Choice, and Premiere.
