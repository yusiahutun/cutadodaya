Larsson &amp;,, Jennings Watches - Emerging Watch Designers

In this digital day and age, the modern timepiece isn't really for telling time anymore. But where function leaves off, fashion picks up. Sleek, simple watches are the new of-the-moment accessory. So wear one for practical purposes, or don't—at least you'll be fashionably late. Read on for the inside scoop on Larsson & Jennings, the Anglo-Swedish brand that's taking the wrists of stylish women everywhere.

What are the words to describe the Larsson & Jennings aesthetic?

Minimalist, contemporary, yet classic.

The brand is described as a hybrid of English and Swedish design/culture. How would you characterize the influence of each?

We try to incorporate clean lines and a contemporary feel to every one of our watches in keeping with Sweden's famously minimalist design approach, which is one of our key inspirations. We add to that features from the classic British dress watch, like sculpted hands and a roman numeral marker, to create a simple and slimline watch suitable for both men and women.

How do the series get their names?

Many are inspired by our Swedish roots - Läder for example, means Leather in Swedish, Kulör means colour.

Which watch style is your most popular right now?

The Läder | Black is our signature and best-selling style as it's so incredibly versatile. Customers have really been stocking up on the CM styles too, often buying both gold and silver to go with their different jewellery options.

Which style is your personal favorite?

I actually have most of our collection but usually switch between three styles : the Läder | Black or the CM | Gold for every day, or the Saxon | Gold when I feel like making a bolder statement.

Who is the quintessential Larsson & Jennings watch wearer?

The beauty of the watches is that they cater for a wide variety of people, so though we design with a 25+ fashion-conscious creative in mind, we're seeing many customers outside that description who react to them just as well. Opening our first brick-and-mortar store in London has shown us the wide range of the customers we have, which is just one of the reasons we hope to open more over the coming year.

What's your style philosophy for accessorizing with a watch? What's the chicest way to wear it?

My watch has now become my main accessory so i chop and change the style to go with what I'm wearing. I'm not one for wearing lots of bracelets so i wear it on its own and let my watch do all the talking. It's amazing to see how many compliments you get.

What makes Larsson & Jennings watches so unique in the market?

The mix of good price, great quality (all watches are Swiss made, with Swiss Ronda movements inside) and minimalist design make them a great understated, statement accessory suitable for people with so many different styles.

What are the key focus points for the brand?

Simple, beautiful and functional are keywords for us. Aesthetic is hugely important but we don't put style over substance; we want to ensure the best possible quality for an accessible price, which is why all our watches are Swiss Made, topped with crystal sapphire glass and teamed with straps handmade from beautiful but hard-wearing leathers we source from tanneries in Sweden and England.

Considering the predominance of smartphones as the go-to time teller, how do you explain the surge in popularity of wristwatches?

Watches used to be either a practical necessity or a status item but with the rise of the smartphone, that necessity has waned. Now, for some people, they're a statement of style instead—just another great fashion accessory to demonstrate one's taste. We try to design for those who actually use them to tell the time, as well as those who don't.

What is next for the brand?

2015 is going to be a great year for us, with more colorways coming to our Classic collections, a handful of exciting collaborations with both UK and USA based brands, two new brick-and-mortar stores and a new series coming out before the end of the year—watch this space.

Sarah Lindig is the senior digital editor, overseeing special projects for Harper's Bazaar.
