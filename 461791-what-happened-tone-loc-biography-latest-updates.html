Tone Loc's biography

Tone Loc is an American rapper, actor, and producer known for his raspy voice. He was born to James Smith and Margaret on March 3, 1966. Tone's father died in 1972, and the rapper and his three siblings were raised by his mother. He took to hanging out in the streets after his father's death when rap music was evolving.

What neighbourhood is Tone Loc from? The rapper was born in Los Angeles, California. He attended the Hollywood Professional School, an exclusive private high school. As a teenager, Tone performed with the Triple-A rap group.

How did Tone start his music career?

Tone Loc got interested in music from an early age. He was barely a teenager, only thirteen years old, when hip hop started becoming big in urban America. Tone started rapping over instrumental passages on records by the Ohio Players, Frankadelic, and other funk groups.

After years of mastering his craft, Tone released his debut album in January 1989 called Lōc-ed After Dark. His first single on the album Wild Thing became a great success and was a national anthem. The song rose to No.2 on the United States Hot 10 and the top twenty in Australia. His second single, Funky Cold Medina, also became a great hit.

Health problems

Tone Loc has had various legal and health issues over the years. The rapper has collapsed on stage several times since 1995. And most of these instances have been about seizures.
