Anne Hathaway reveals her bucket list includes making 'as many memories as possible' with her family

Anne Hathaway makes a striking cover star for Vogue Hong Kong's November 2022 issue.

The longtime actress, 39, revealed inside the pages of the glossy that one of her bucket list goals is to make 'as many memories as possible' with her family.

The star is married to Adam Shulman and the two share sons Jonathan Rosebanks Shulman, six, and Jack Shulman, two.

Gorgeous: Anne Hathaway makes a striking cover star for Vogue Hong Kong's November 2022 issue

The stunning cover and interview were shared on Friday, via Vogue HK's Instagram account.

Along with one of the snapshots by photographer Dan Jackson, one post read, 'After making her big break in “The Princess Diaries”, and the now iconic film, “The Devil Wears Prada”, the Oscar winning actress has proven herself to be Hollywood's power elite by taking on a variety of roles.'

For the remarkable images Anne was decked out in jewelry by Giorgio Armani and BVLGARI, styled by Dena Giannini.

Her luscious brunette hair was swept up into a voluminous fountain ponytail that cascaded down her back. 

Opening up: The longtime actress, 39, revealed inside the pages of the glossy that one of her bucket list goals is to make 'as many memories as possible' with her family

Although Hathaway's career has remained a priority since she became a mother, the movie star is still focused on the simple things in life.

When asked what's next as she prepares to turn 40, she answered: 'Surprise myself as much as possible. Surf more. Get a decent camera and use it often. Make as many memories with my family as possible.'

Her newest project, the historical drama Armageddon Time directed by James Gray, was released October 28.

And she'll next star in Eileen, a film adaptation based on the novel of the same name.

Hathaway has also recently been spotted working on another film set as she worked on Mothers' Instinct alongside Jessica Chastain.

'That was the hardest role I have played,' she told the fashion magazine. 'It touched my worst fear and I almost backed out of the film because I didn’t know if I could go there as an actress.'

Nevertheless, the talented performer prevailed, committing to the role and serving as one of the producers of the movie.

In the feature she and Chastain play neighbors and close friends whose idyllic lives are turned upside down after a tragic accident, according to Deadline.
