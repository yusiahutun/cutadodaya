Dash cam footage shows cops 'sack' OK QB Baker Mayfield

Dash cam footage has captured the moment an Oklahoma Sooners quarterback was tackled by cops into a concrete wall after he tried to flee from police officers while drunk.

Baker Mayfield was arrested in Fayetteville, Arkansas on February 25 on misdemeanor complaints of public intoxication, disorderly conduct, resisting arrest and fleeing.

Footage from the dashboard camera of a cop car shows the 21-year-old being interviewed by police following an incident at a restaurant that involved Mayfield.

An officer is heard shouting over to the quarterback: 'Hey, dude, if you don't keep your mouth down, you're going to jail right now, OK? If I hear you cuss one more time, yell at her.'

Mayfield agreed to stop, telling the officer: 'OK, I'm done. I'm done. I'm done.'

But moments later he started walking away from the officers, before breaking into a run.

Before he could get more than a few steps away, he was tackled to ground by two cops, appearing to hit his head on the adjacent concrete wall as he went down.

'Oh god, I'm done. I'm done,' Mayfield shouted as he was pinned to the ground by the officers. 

'You are right. You are f***ing done,' one cop told him.

'Are you kidding me?' Mayfield responded. 'I'm done.'

The police ordered Mayfield to put his hands behind his back.

'It's behind my back,' Mayfield insisted, sounded tearful. 'I didn't say anything. Are you kidding me? Are you kidding me? I didn't say anything.'

'No, you're just a dumbass who tried to run,' an officer responded.

Later the player, who came in third for the 2016 Heisman Trophy, asked a cop 'What did I do wrong?' to which they replied, 'Well, you were yelling. And then when I told you to stop, you took off running.'

Oklahoma quarterback Baker Mayfield was arrested for drunkenly shouting at police and resisting arrest on February 25

The video ends with some of the officers mocking Mayfield after learning he played for Oklahoma. 

'By the way he's the quarterback for OU,' an officer told his colleague.

'He is... Is he really? He's not very fast.'

A police report obtained by ESPN  said that Mayfield had slurred speech and food was spilled down the front of his clothing when he was interviewed by police in the early hours of February 25.

He was arrested and booked into county jail in Fayetteville, Arkansas, where he spent three hours before he was granted bail to return for his hearing date on April 7. 

Mayfield has since issued an apology on Twitter saying he'd made the 'biggest mistake of my life.'

'I know that I represent the University of Oklahoma on a very large scale, therefore I know better than to bring bad attention and scrutiny to the school and people that I love,' he wrote. 'The shame, guilt and embarrassment I have felt over the past few days and continue to feel is something I would never wish upon anybody.'

He also apologized to his coaches and teammates saying, 'I am sorry for not being the leader you know I am.'

Mayfield (left on the field and right with his girlfriend) was released from jail on bond three hours after being booked

The 21-year-old will appear in court to face charges including public intoxication and resisting arrest on April 7

Oklahoma University said in a statement shortly after the arrest: 'We are aware of the matter. We don't have any other information at this time.' 

Mayfield began his college football career as a walk-on player for the Texas Tech Red Raiders where he became the first freshman to start a season opener at a BCS school.

He later transferred to Oklahoma following what he called a lack of communication with coaches. After sitting out the 2014 season due to NCAA transfer rules, Mayfield won the starting quarterback job in 2015 and has finished in the top five of the Heisman the past two seasons.

He also broke the Football Bowl Subdivision season passing-efficiency record last season and received the most outstanding player trophy for his team's victory against the Auburn Tigers in the Sugar Bowl.
