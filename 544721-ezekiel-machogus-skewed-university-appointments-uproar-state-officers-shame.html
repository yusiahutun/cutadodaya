Ezekiel Machogu's Skewed University Appointments Causes Uproar: &quot;State Officers Have No Shame&quot;

  • Education Cabinet Secretary Ezekiel Machogu has found himself at the centre of a storm over his appointments
  • Lawyer Donald Kipkorir criticised the CS for his actions, sharing a page of the gazette notice detailing various appointments
  • Most who reacted to Kipkorir's post stated that there was a clear lack of regional and ethnic balance in his appointments

Didacus Malowa, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings over three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Nairobi - Education Cabinet Secretary Ezekiel Machogu has come under criticism online following his latest university appointments.

In a post shared on X, flamboyant city lawyer Donald Kipkorir called out the CS for what he claimed to be favouritism on the CS's part.

The lawyer explained that state officers have been able to getaway with such skewed appointments because of no consequences.

Robert Alai slams CS Machogu

In a separate incident, Kileleshwa MCA Robert Alai slammed Machogu's remarks over the Nyanza region's two operational factories.

Machogu claimed the region contributed less than 1% to the development of the country's manufacturing sector.

Refuting his claims, Alai listed close to 10 factories distributed across the Nyanza region, questioning the research done by the CS.

Alai dismissed the remarks, adding that CS could know better by virtue of his sitting in the Executive arm of government.

He listed key factories in Kisumu, Migori and Homa Bay counties, including the Getonganya Sweet Potato Processing Factory in Kuria.
